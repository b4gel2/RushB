Autonomous Vehicles are looking to become the way of the future; with cars having self-driving functions that look only to improve the way we live our lives. To put it simply an autonomous vehicle is one that can get you from point A to point B with little to no human input. As technology advances autonomous vehicles are becoming more and more realistic, especially with a lot of new cars these days having strong autonomous features, mainly being pushed by brands like Tesla. The main driving force behind autonomous vehicles and where all the growth is occurring is in the car industry. Car brands like Tesla have been working on making cars autonomous from their inception. All current Tesla have the feature called lane departure avoidance where once set in a driving lane the car senses where the lane strips are and will keep the car central in the lane. It also has an autopilot mode where drivers can sit back and take their hands off the wheel using this technology to ensure the car stays within the lane and basically drives it until the driver needs to take control in moving lanes or slowing down. Google is also making grounds in the area with their own fully functioning self-driving car called WAYMO. What started as a Google project of having a car using live Google maps street view to guide itself around the campus is now its own self-driving company. To put it simply WAYMO is a self-driving Uber service only located in Phoenix, Arizona, where guests can book a WAYMO via the ride-sharing app. It works exactly the same as Uber or a Taxi just with the difference that there is no one in the drivers seat. The state of the art technology is still being worked out but basically it is possible in current days for a car to drive from point A to point B in traffic without assistance or a driver, however it has not been introduced so heavily to the market as there still is the risk of accidents with there being a few deaths recorded due to self driving features, such as tesla autopilot. The future for autonomous vehicles, however, is really exciting with the technology only improving. Within the next 3 years the possibilities are endless, with there to only be more and more self driving cars on the roads each day. Uber is investing a lot of money into self-driving technology with the hopes of one day removing drivers from there all there cars and being run purely off self driving technology. Within the last 24 hours Uber has actually begun to start mapping out the streets of the city of Dallas as it begins to slowly roll out its self-driving car features within the city for a test demo. If successful in Dallas, Uber plan on mapping a whole range of cities before rolling out self-driving cars worldwide. The science behind self-driving cars is very impressive and is a step up from the basics we’ve had in cars for years, such as with assistive parking and the automatic self brake system. It is these basics along with new innovations such as blind spot monitoring and forward collision warnings that create the basics for self-driving. With new cars having sensors to sense all these it is easy to understand how a car could operate without a driver, all the new technology is just to ensure safety on the roads by creating technology that follows the rules of the roads and can operate through tough conditions like turning in heavy traffic e.c.t. The growth in these sensors mixed with the mapping systems used in Google maps that assist-creating routes for the cars to follow is the modern technology propelling this futuristic idea to fruition.

One of the most state-of-the-art implementations of cyber security is The CyberArk Digital Vault created by a team of security engineers from CyberArk, a world leading cyber security agency designed with several security features and configurations that help to minimize the attack surface of individual host servers. Hence, account information will be securely stored. The Digital Vault technology engulfs data with eight layers of security within a present network framework, forming a repository to withhold confidential data which acts a digital safe. A safe may hold many files however all files are encrypted with a unique file encryption key and a different safe encryption key which is inimitable to the safe. The vault stores all the safe encryption keys and the keys are encrypted with an individual vault encryption key. Individuals only have access to the keys based on whether the individual has the appropriate access rights. Access to the safe is classified by administrators who work at CyberArk and data within the safe may only be accessed under supervision from a Safe Supervisor. CyberArk’s Digital Vault is continually being updated and upgraded with the latest technologies and hardware to prevail upon other cybersecurity companies to gain the most satisfied customers with their product.

There are cybersecurity measures which can be sought by individuals at a reasonable cost which have great efficiency and effectiveness such as VPN’s. A VPN allows the user's initial IP address to be replaced with one from the Virtual Private Network provider, this lets users to have information such as their location hidden or misleading. Furthermore, VPN’s also encrypts data sent between the Network of the user and the VPN server. VPN’s have also been used to illegally download movies, games and tv shows by individuals using torrents.
